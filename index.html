📊 Canonical Correspondence Analysis (CCA)

Biplot showing relationships between water quality parameters and organism abundance

📌 How to Read the CCA Biplot

  • Blue circles represent sample sites (water samples)
  • Red arrows represent environmental variables (water parameters)
  • Green arrows represent organism species
  • Arrow length indicates strength of relationship
  • Arrow direction shows correlation pattern
  • Organisms near environmental arrows are positively associated

🔬 Principal Component Analysis (PCA)

Ordination showing overall variation patterns in combined environmental and biological data

📌 How to Read the PCA Biplot

  • Blue circles represent sample sites
  • Red arrows represent environmental variables
  • Green arrows represent organism species
  • PC1 and PC2 capture the most variation in the dataset
  • Distance between sites reflects overall similarity
  • Variables pointing in similar directions are correlated
